Dogma was replaced with dogma.
Absolute fire-and-brimstone turned to absolute peace-and-love[^1].
History was more nuanced than that soundbite, of course.
The Church saw wrath as a sin[^2], but equally, and possibly more so, saw a lack of justified anger as a sin.
Christ flipped the tables of the money-changers.
Passion was treated as a gift when set to well-ordered ends.
The abject passivity that is totally mandated of the office eunuch in all of life is viewed as unjust and inhuman.
Offic(ial) creed: Don't let humanity get in the way of the machine.

[^1]: For "our" team at least.
[^2]: A translation of "sin" to the parlance of the bugman model of morality: "yuck".
      [Ayer](https://archive.org/details/AlfredAyer/mode/2up?view=theater) would agree with this for the wrong reason.
