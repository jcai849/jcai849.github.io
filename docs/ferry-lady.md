---
title: The Old Lady on the Ferry
date: 2022-05-13
---

"But you know the relation between mathematics and music?"

"Sure," I said, "to an extent. There are ratios in chords and progressions, and certain properties of frequencies, things like that."

"Yes. And they have this instrument that can see music. This man, [name forgotten], invented it - it's more than an oscilloscope and it is essential to semotics."

"Oh - semotics. I thought you'd said mathematics"

I thought about it - semotics.. like semiotics? Signs, etc.? There would be some relation to music no doubt, but I'm interested in hearing her relate the two.
She smiled and lightly touched my arm.

"Yes. C - Y - M. Cymotics."

This sounded to me like that brain-probing instrument they use in scientology.
She continued; "It's more than music, it's the sound itself that's important. Every cell in your body, yourself as a cell in the world, is responsive to acoustics. Sound is everywhere and you have to get the right sounds."

I took her bait, knowing that she was guaranteed to have opinions well beyond the tired mainstream. A penny for her thoughts on the state of modern music.

"Does electronic music make this easier? Or does something like, say, classical music, possess more of the right sounds?"

Her response was obvious. Of course not electronic. She went on:
"Classical music, with some composers more than others of course, was able to point at some of the better acoustic patterns. But one important thing is to have the instrument itself there. You can't capture it in a recording."

I smiled, taking her in.
Her mask was nowhere to be seen, and I was glad of the implicit invitation to remove mine.
She had an elegant manner, verging on debonaire.
I would have placed her in her mid-sixties, though I admit to my possession of a flatterer's bias.
Her accent was impossible to place.
If we were in America I'd describe it as transatlantic.
Here, perhaps an accent hinting at a youth in England, probably some place with money.

She picked up the conversation, now bolder.

"Sounds nowadays are terrible, you can't escape. There are so many poor acoustics, and the worst ones are invisible. Phones emit a sound and a particular heat that's no good for your organs. You hold it up to your head," she paused, miming the action, "and it slowly burns away your brain. Pregnant women, carrying their phones right by their baby..."

She broke off, as the consequences were too dire to speak aloud.
