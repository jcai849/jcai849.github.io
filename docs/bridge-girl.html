I Don’t Believe the Figure of 700 a Year

Jason Cairns

2022-06-09

Frame 1
I’m looking out of the passenger window at a girl dressed all in white, walking down the mossy sidewalk of Grafton Road. Caroline asks, “What’s that girl doing?,” in a panicked tone, and I know she’s referring to the other girl; the one standing on the concrete barrier of the bridge. I am about to explain to her that she’s just watching traffic.
Frame 2
The car is 10 metres ahead of where it was in frame 1, and I don’t voice any explanation. The girl is lifting her leg over the railing.
Frame 3
The car is slowing down, Caroline is shouting, “Go! Go! Go!” to me.
Frame 4
I hear my door open and look down. The car isn’t moving quickly, but the ground still seems to be. There’s the sound of a car horn from across the road.
Frame 5
I’m halfway across the broad sidewalk. She’s got both legs over.
Frame 6
I wrap my arms around her and pull back.
Frame 7
She lands, and I keep a tight grip on her, both of us sitting on the ground.
Frame 8
I notice that she’s barefoot. She stares ahead at infinity, with no tears or fear. Her mascara looks a day old and clumped. Caroline passes my jacket and I offer it as a blanket.
Frame 9
The girl in white comes and grips her as well. No one says anything.

Motion resumes. Caroline calls the police, and a man from nearby helps her clarify and finish the call. The girl in white asks the other one her name and that kind of thing. The girl is looking around nervously for police, and I tell her not to worry, we’re not leaving. A lady carrying a shopping bag sees the girl and us holding her, and cries and hugs her. She says, “I’ve been there. I’ve been there. It’s okay.” I loosen my grip while they embrace. Police eventually arrive. The lady with the shopping bag leaves. A man walking by tells me that the same thing happened to him when he was a student, just one bridge over, where he found someone about to jump. The lady with the shopping bag runs back and hands the girl some knitted item from the bag. I hope it’s not a scarf, but it looks more like slippers. No one knows how to part. The police take a statement from me. We drive off.
