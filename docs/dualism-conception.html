Conception of Dualism

Jason Cairns

2022-05-03

The dualism of conceivability of mind-body separation underlies the conceivability of dualism of mind-body

Metaphysical conceivability of a distinction is sufficient to demonstrate non-identicality of the objects of said distinction.

A mind without a body is conceivable[1].

A body without a mind is conceivable[2].

Mind is not physically brain in some possible world. Therefore mind is not metaphysically brain in every world.

Subjectivity and privacy of mind distinguish it from any other physical thing in the world.

Experience is it’s own knowledge. Unconscious direction or intention equally and more compellingly so. Intention provides truth values to primitive propositions, content is provided by the mind.

Appearance is it’s own reality. Nothing else shares such direct apprehension.

Incoherence and confusion are not proof of non-atomicity of awareness. The subject is the unique one. The experience is singular and unified.

Better described as form and matter.
